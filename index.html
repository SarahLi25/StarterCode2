Let's Talk Health

From tanning safety to mental health, discover key health concerns young adults face today. Learn how to protect your body and mind with awareness and smart choices.

Tanning


Causes

  1. Frequent use of tanning beds
  2. Unprotected sun exposure
  3. Misconceptions about "safe" tanning


Effects

  1. Early skin aging, such as leathery skin and wrinkles
  2. Iron deficiency anemia
  3. Eye damage form UV exposure


Tips to Improve

  1. Use SPF 30+ every day
  2. Avoid indoor tanning
  3. Wear protectice clothing and sunglasses


Stats

Indoor tanning before age 35 increases melanoma risk by 75%, American Academy of Dermatology

STDs (Sexually Transmitted Diseases)


Causes

  1. Unprotected Sex
  2. Lack of regular STD testing
  3. Misinformation or stigma


Effects

  1. Long term infertility problems
  2. Increased risk of contracting HIV
  3. Chronic pelvic pain


Tips to Improve

  1. Get tested every 3-6 months if sexually active
  2. Use condoms and dental dams
  3. Get vaccinated for HPV and Hepatitis B


Stats

Youth ages 15-24 account for nearly 50% of all new STD infections each year, CDC STD Data

Mental Health


Causes

  1. School, college, and job pressure
  2. social media and cyberbullying"
  3. Isolation and lack of support


Effects

  1. Trouble focusing or sleeping
  2. Chronic fatigue
  3. Withdrawl from social situations
  4. Risk of self harm or suicide


Tips to Improve

  1. Seek professional therapy
  2. Build a strong support system
  3. Take breaks from social media or places that may cause it


Stats

Over 50% of youth report symptoms related to anxiety or depression. Suicid eis th e2nd leading cause of death in people aged 15-24, NIMH

Colon Cancer


Causes

  1. Low fiber, high processed meat diets
  2. Sedentary Lifestyles
  3. Obesity and smoking


Effects

  1. Abdominal pain or rectal bleeding
  2. Iron deficiency anemia
  3. If undiagnosed, can spread and get worse


Tips to Improve

  1. Eat more fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
  2. Exercise regularly
  3. Limit slcohole and stop smoking
  4. Talk to your doctor about early screenings if you are at risk


Stats

Rates of colorectal cancer in people under 50 have risen by 50% since 1994, Amaerican Cancer Society

Eating Disorder


Causes

  1. Perfectionism or trauma
  2. Cultrual pressure from social media to be thin and skinny to be "desirable"
  3. Low self esteem


Effects

  1. Malnutrion, fainting, low heart rate
  2. Organ Damage, especially heart
  3. Increased risk of depression, suicide, and substance abuse


Tips to Improve

  1. Seek professional help
  2. Practice body positivity
  3. Avoid diet talk or body shaming in conversations


Stats

35-37% of adolescent girls engage in crash dieting, fasting, or other disordered behaviors, National Eating Disorders Association

Poor Diets


Causes

  1. Fast Food Convenience
  2. Lack of nutrition education"
  3. Emotional eating, such as stress binging


Effects

  1. Obesity, type 2 diabetes
  2. Digestive problems
  3. Low performance levels in energy and academics


Tips to Improve

  1. Replace sugary drinks such as soda with water
  2. Try home cooked meals or meal prep
  3. Learn to read nutrition labels and plan snacks


Stats

Fewer than 10% of Americans eat the recommended amount of fruits and vegetables daily, CDC Nutrition
